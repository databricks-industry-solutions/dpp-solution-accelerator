{# Pipeline stages #}

Pipeline stages — Bronze → Silver → Gold

Bronze

Streaming snapshot of the Lakebase tables (bronze_manufacturer, bronze_supplier, bronze_product_passport…). Append-only, full history via Delta time travel.

Silver

Conformance plus data-quality expectations (valid country codes, non-null keys, in-range scores). Bad rows are dropped or flagged, not silently kept.

Gold

Business tables: gold_passport_complete, gold_supply_chain_lineage, gold_carbon_by_category, gold_compliance_gap_analysis, gold_circularity_metrics.

    return dlt.read_stream("bronze_manufacturer")

Declarative Python — you state what each table should contain and its quality rules; Databricks handles orchestration, lineage, and serverless compute. Pipelines run serverless; Gold can be Delta-Shared to partners without copying a row.

{# Governance #}

Governance — Unity Catalog

  • •Audit trail by trigger: every insert/update/delete on the operational tables is captured in passport_audit_log automatically.
  • •End-to-end lineage: one governed catalog spans the operational (Lakebase), analytical (Delta), and AI layers, so you can trace any Gold metric back to source.
  • •Access control: the demo grants to PUBLIC for simplicity; in production you scope least-privilege grants per app service principal (viewer = read, portal = write).

{# Key points #}
  • •The EU DPP system is decentralized: each operator stores and serves its own passport data; the EU registry holds only high-level metadata. Databricks is the governed data foundation under any DPP front-end.
  • •The hard part is not storage or the QR code — it is sourcing, quality, and governing data across the multi-tier supply chain.
  • •One domain-agnostic schema, selectable profiles. Battery is the default; deploy with --var profile=furniture to serve another sector from the same foundation.
